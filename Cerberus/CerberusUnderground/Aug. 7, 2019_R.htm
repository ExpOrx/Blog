From Aug. 7, 2019, to Aug. 20, 2019, the actor ANDROID posted the following Cerberus Android banking bot updates at the XSS forum:

On Aug. 7, 2019:
--- 

Dear current and future customers!

I will answer the most frequently asked questions so that I don't have to reply to everyone personally:
Q: Sometimes the bot doesn't have admin privileges on a device. Why are they needed and what do they do?
A: Admin rights are required only when you want to block the device. Deletion of the app is prevented once accessibility rights are granted.

Q: How do I call an inject for a specific app? I can't do it.
A: An inject can be displayed only if the app it is made for is installed on the victim's device. I'll explain why. Let's say you're using your phone, and all of a sudden, a PayPal login window is displayed even though you don't use PayPal. This is at the very least suspicious.

Q: Will injects for Anubis and other bots work?
A: No! But they can be adapted. Our bot loads and stores all injects locally, meaning they don't load anything from third-party websites. So all scripts, styles and pictures need to be injected into the HTML code. Moreover, we will provide the ordinary JS API for transferring data from the inject.

Q: Google Play Protect doesn't allow installing the APK; it flags it as malicious. What can I do?
A: Use APK crypting services. We will send you the full list of crypting services and a few invites for them. We will offer our own crypter soon.

Moreover, we are working on an update that would allow customers to time specific actions, such as disabling Play Protect, gaining admin rights and other minor permissions.

Manual on developing web injects for Malware Cerberus.

Web injects for Cerberus are HTML files that contain CSS and JS code.
The web injects are downloaded to a device and work locally. They don't have Internet access, so there is NO option to download anything (images or scripts) from the Internet! You should add everything to the HTML file. Add scripts as plain text, and add images using base64.

The Android.returnResult function that receives a valid JSON [file] is used by the web inject to send information about entered data.
In our sample web injects you can see how this function is used. If you want to stop the web inject after it's finished working, you should send the "exit":"" parameter into the JSON [file], because it shows the bot that everything's been collected by the web inject and that it shouldn't be shown again.

If you have any additional questions, please contact the support reps.
We will provide sample web injects.

This report serves as an Activity Update on the actor ANDROID, whom we have been tracking as the actor behind the Android banking bot dubbed Cerberus (see: Similar Reports section).


Information from our source and possible customers

On Aug. 25, 2019, our sensitive and reliable source contacted the threat actor via private messages on the XSS forum and learned the following additional details about the Android malware:

— The actor stressed an inject generator was implemented, therefore, the Cerberus Android bot would be shipped out-of-the-box without any web-injects. Clients can add web-injects themselves or order development from third-party services.
— The actor ANDROID confirmed a custom virtual network computing (VNC) module was developed and testing started. However, the actor further reported the product with the VNC solution probably would not be sold publicly.
— The actor claimed some restrictions for new customers were implemented. According to the actor, the changes were made because a large number of potential customers know little to nothing about Android malware.

The the actor’s customers have increased in number, which is evident from positive feedback on the product left at the Exploit and XSS forums. Based on these reviews, we estimate the actor sold the malware to at least eight users, including:

— Those who provided positive reviews at the Exploit forum included the actors stan86, st1x0, m_vs_m and Sparteam.
— Those XSS forum members who shared their thoughts and experience with the product in the subject thread included the actors goidini, PokemonGo, RonaldRuck and demencukigor49.

The actor’s product was reviewed by a former administrator of the Russian-language cybercrime forum Damagelab (aka XSS), the highly respected actor Ar3s (see: https://titan.intel471.com/post_thread/2582a190a3d63b88d9a60294a9898af9). According to Ar3s, the actor tested the malware and believed it performed well and as described. It is likely this review added credibility to the service and/or contributed to an increase in sales.


Activity update

Since our previous report, ANDROID’s activity at the XSS forum primarily was related to promoting the Android malware and answering questions about Cerberus features and performance.

Our research also revealed the actor’s presence expanded to the Exploit forum, which the actor joined July 3, 2019, under the alternative handle ANDROID-Cerberus. Reviewing the actor’s advertisement thread at the forum, we observed the forum moderator confirmed the actor made a deposit to the forum’s escrow service of 0.1920 bitcoin (about US $2,000 at the time of this report). The actor also received a “Seller” status on the board. On Aug. 15, 2019, the moderator claimed the account of ANDROID-Cerberus was suspended for suspicion of running multiple accounts, but at the time of this report, the actor’s account already was active.

Reading through the posts made in the Android bot advertisement thread, we discovered a prominent Android malware developer, the actor Sochi, claimed to have received a malware sample for testing and agreed to publish a personal opinion on it afterward. However, there was no review from Sochi at the time of this report.


Speculation that ANDROID is a scammer

Although the actor’s Android bot primarily collected positive feedback from clients, several actors complained ANDROID might be offering to sell a slightly reworked version of other malware, such as the Black Rose Lucy Android bot.

The initial complaint was filed by the actor benbin77 at the Exploit forum (see: https://titan.intel471.com/post_thread/fc34001a377d88000b4a3a0bce40f435). The actor benbin77 suspected ANDROID was running multiple accounts and mentioned mem0, DEADC0DE, PokemonGo, sparteam and FreeWar as ANDROID's alternative personas.

The actor FreeWar mentioned above has been on the underground scene for a while and appeared on our radar under different online personas. In mid-February 2019, we reported the actor offered to sell an Android bot and a binder to join malware with legitimate Android applications at the Exploit and XSS forums. According to our previous research, we also believe FreeWar is an alternative online persona of the actor 0xd3ad, an author of the Asacub Android malware (see: https://titan.intel471.com/report/854362c0e666193b927d838afea9c937).

Nevertheless, the actor FreeWar was banned from the forums for allegedly targeting victims in the Commonwealth of Independent States (CIS). This is where the actor benbin77’s complaint supports our theory that FreeWar still is active, but working under an alternative moniker.

The second complaint was posted by the actor Ar3s at the XSS forum. According to the complaint, the actors benbin77 at the Jabber ID benibn77@exploit.im and rello at the Jabber ID rello@exploit.im expressed their negative attitude toward the actor ANDROID. While the actor benbin77’s claims were similar as the aforementioned, rello claimed the Cerberus Android bot was a redeveloped version of the actor’s private Android malware dubbed Black Rose Lucy Android bot. The actor claimed the product was developed at the request of rello’s client and the source code never was leaked.

It is interesting that rello’s claims support our previous research from early October 2018. We already reported about the Black Rose Lucy Android malware and associated this bot with the actor cyberbob (aka Kwanza, a, j3r0nim0 ). According to the actor’s posts, cyberbob acknowledged ordering the bot and claimed rello was improving and developing features for the actor’s private Android malware (see: https://titan.intel471.com/report/d7bb495d83dcd0f5c3eae756ceb1116b).

As a result of the aforementioned complaint, Ar3s and the actor 2c71e9, who helped to compare the Cerberus Android bot and rello’s bot, concluded it was unlikely the Cerberus Android bot was based on the source code of Black Rose Lucy. However, the forum administrator commented that the source code of the two bots must be compared for a 100 percent accurate conclusion, and that likely is impossible. The actor ANDROID dodged a question about maintaining possible multiple accounts across the forums and claimed they operate in a team, therefore, it stands to reason they may have several accounts. The actor finished the post with the phrase “Regards, Cerberus team,” which leads us to believe at least some accounts mentioned by benbin77, such as DEADC0DE, PokemonGo, sparteam and FreeWar, might be associated with a collective behind the Cerberus bot. The complaint was closed by the forum administrator Aug. 12, 2019, as groundless.
