Actor ANDROID advertises Cerberus Android banking bot

25 Jun 2019 19:07:02 UTC

The Android malware market has been stagnant recently and only a small number of products were available. In April 2019, we reported the actor android (aka Exosales, Exosales2 ) started selling the Android banking trojan Exobot Compact (see: https://titan.intel471.com/report/fd8a595b1e65876103809c23f60e0390). Another vendor, the actor obf (aka Bestoffer ), has been active with the semiprivate Gustuff Android banking trojan (see: https://titan.intel471.com/report/917aa326a8625371067a826d0c5d87c1).

Several actors claimed to be operating or developing private Android bots. For example, the actor trafimer allegedly was running an unspecified private product (see: https://titan.intel471.com/report/caca7dab6d51fa78a7db56cefd805271). A former supporter of the Anubis Android bot, the actor GEDeON (aka Gedeon ), allegedly planned to start developing a new version of Anubis for private use only. Several actors offered leaked versions of the Anubis Android bot.

Although new malware strains always are in the underground community spotlight, discussion of the Cerberus Android bot was limited thus far. The actor ANDROID shared screenshots of the control panel two days before the official sales started, when the actor commented in the actor Xrenovi4’s thread and asked about recent trends in the Android malware area. The actor Xrenovi4 subsequently provided a positive review of the Cerberus control panel and functionality.  

Another forum member, the actor ndaloz, also provided positive feedback about the Cerberus bot and claimed the malware previously was only for private use. The actor ndaloz’s post likely indicates the actor used or tested the bot. The actor ndaloz personally was involved in web hacking and selling access to compromised websites and payment card databases (see: https://titan.intel471.com/report/ba97749fe862c5e851e36f28845b7239).

The Cerberus Android bot control panel roughly resembles the control panel of the Anubis bot and several actors also noticed the similarity. The bot’s logo is an image of a three-headed dog and resembles a forum avatar used by the actor CC_CERBER. However, it seems to be a coincidence.

Information from our source

On June 24, 2019, our sensitive and reliable source contacted the actor ANDROID and obtained additional details about the bot, including features, price tags and a list of web-injects. The actor stressed the malware was developed from scratch and previously was available for private use only.  

1. According to the actor, the Cerberus Android bot had the following functionality at the time of this report (translated from Russian):

--- 

<…>

Cerberus Android BOT
Features:
— Modular structure
— Sending SMS
— Intercepting SMS
— Invisible SMS interception
— Locking the device
— Switching to silent mode
— Keylogger (messengers, WatsApp, secret chats on Telegram, banking, etc.; not enabled in browsers)
— Running USSD codes
— Forwarding calls
— Force closing phishing tabs of the apps (banks and the like)
— Running any installed application
— Push notifications from apps (A custom push notification from any app can be triggered)
— Opening URLs in the browser
— Obtaining the list of all the apps that are installed on a device
— Obtaining the contact list
— Obtaining all the SMS messages
— Deleting any application
— Deleting itself
— Prohibiting users from deleting the bot
— Prohibiting users from lowering admin privileges
— Prohibiting users from disabling Accessibility Services
— Automatically accepting rights and permissions
— The option to have several reserve URLs for connecting to the server (They can be dynamically set up in the admin panel)
— Web injects (HTML+JS+CSS; they will be downloaded to a device and then run from the drive; they will not be affected by bad connection or no connection at all)
— CC grabber
— Email grabber
— Automatically running web injects after the time specified in the admin panel elapses
— Automatically disabling Google Play Protect + setting up the timeout for it in the admin panel
— Anti-emulator (The bit starts working only after spotting some device activity)
Injects and (Invisible) SMS interception are available in versions 5 to 9; looking forward to version 10 =)
<…>

The impacted enterprises included AT&T, Bank of America, Capital One, Discover, Fifth Third Bank, JPMorgan Chase, USAA, US Bank and Wells Fargo.

The actor  ANDROID claimed a client would be able to add custom web-injects developed by third-party services through the JavaScript application programming interface (API) in the control panel.

3. According to the actor, it is possible to rent the bot for three, six or 12 months and prices range from US $4,000 to US $12,000 (translated from Russian):

--- 

<…>

We will be selling the three-, six- and 12-month licenses for using the bot. They will cost US $4,000, US $7,000 and US $12,000, respectively.

The license covers free updates and support. The updates also include web-injects, which will be added for other banks whenever necessary.

Assessment of credibility

The actor ANDROID recently resurfaced on the market, so the malware lacked any extensive reviews at the time of this report. However, the first clients who tested or used the bot provided positive responses. The actor ANDROID also gave our source additional details about the product. Therefore, we assess the information posted by the actor with a Credibility of “2 — Probably True.”
