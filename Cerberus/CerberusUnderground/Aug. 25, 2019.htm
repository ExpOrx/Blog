This is an Activity Update on the actor Validolik (aka Validol-hack, Niko777, ChestoooR ), whom we have been tracking since May 2017, as a prolific vendor of web-injects for major mobile malware strains (see: Similar Reports section).

 

Information from our source

On Aug. 26, 2019, our sensitive and reliable source reached the actor Validolik at the Telegram ID @val1dolik and collected the following updates:

— The actor tailored web-injects for the Anubis Android bot and reported they can be customized for any Android malware on the market, including the recently announced Cerberus Android banking bot.

— The actor offered a service to set up the Anubis Android bot and install web-injects into the bot control panel for clients who purchased a subscription.

— The actor provided our source with a link to a private Telegram chat room at @validol1k catering to the actor’s clients and promised to post updates and important announcements there. Our sensitive and reliable source gained insight into the channel and reported 39 members were registered, which can be considered the approximate number of clients. Our source was unable to provide a list of members since their IDs were hidden.

— According to our source, the actor Validolik was short of funds and offered to sell a subscription for the full pack of web-injects for US $1,100, with roughly a 25-percent discount.

Through the course of the current research, we confirmed the results of our previous analysis which identified Validolik as the possible Armenian national Самвел Марданян (Eng. Samvel Mardanyan ). Our source obtained and provided the actor’s Telegram ID avatar that depicts a man holding a ball in front of a basketball hoop (see: Attachment 004.png). The same photo was used in Самвел Марданян’s VKontakte (VK) Russian social media networking profile at vk.com/bcaa3 (see: Attachment 005.png). 

 

The actor’s recent activity
The actor Validolik’s recent update suggests the Anubis Android bot remains a popular tool for threat actors attacking mobile users, even though its author, the actor maza-in, abandoned the project allegedly after an arrest in March 2019. For example, the actor DukeEugene acknowledged using Validolik’s web-injects with a modified Anubis bot, which has a Google Play Protect circumvention mechanism implemented. We recently reported the actor Constantin offered such malware (see: https://titan.intel471.com/report/a047407fcd2863b3ea614961a6537df7). However, it is unclear whether DukeEugene is Constantin’s customer.

Since our previous report, Validolik demonstrated a low level of activity and primarily promoted the actor’s own web-inject development service. A review of the actor’s advertisement thread revealed Validolik’s new clients included the actors Kirilka232, DukeEugene, Archiduc, Alkazar and princesteven.

Through the course of our research, we also identified a complaint filed by Validolik against the actor stronholdem at the Jabber ID barmik@xmpp.org.uk, who allegedly owed Validolik US $400 as payment for a subscription. As a result, stronholdem was banned from the forum.

According to the most recent update, the subscription for the actor’s service includes 186 web-injects impacting organizations in 25 countries, and five web-injects targeting social media and financial applications.
