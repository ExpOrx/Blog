Actor crbr is a new member of the Russian language cyber crime forum exploit.in having joined the board on Feb 24, 2016. The actor has demonstrated a rather high level of activity having made 52 posts. The actor has a “Seller” status at the board. Analysis of the actor’s posts indicates that his activity was largely confined to advertising of the Cerber Ransomware and discussing topics around it.

The following is an overview of other posts of interest made by the actor:

- May 21, 2016 – was looking for a “high-quality” FastFlux service. This post was made right after the one described below and was later deleted;
- May 21, 2016 – complained in the sales thread of actor AltHost (the actor is known to provide bullet proof hosting services, including FastFlux; please see linked report) that he had problems with name servers that left some of his web sites non-operational – a strong indicator that the Cerber Ransomware is/was operating via AltHost’s infrastructure. This post was later deleted;
- May 19, 2016 – was inquiring about joining other cyber crime boards, including on a paid basis – an indicator the actor is seeking ways to expand his client list.

 

In his follow-up posts in the sales thread the actor claimed the ransomware comes bundled with an unspecified LPE exploit that allows one to bypass UAC request under Windows 7 – 8.1 (x32, x64); target system’s running under Windows Vista and Windows 10 are flooded with UAC requests in effort to make the victim run the malware. He has also shared links to several video footages, demonstrating malware’s operation on a test system (all footages may be found attached to this report).
