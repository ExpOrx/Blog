Actor lamba (aka lam, pronka, krot1, Prospero) engaged in OpenToYou ransomware campaign; Cooperates with underground RDP shops, SOCKS proxy vendors and developers of malware and hacking tools

06 Jun 2017 20:20:13 UTC
{ "tab": "raw" } Raw text { "tab": "comments" } Researcher comments

We have recently reported that actor lamba (aka lam, pronka, krot1, Prospero ), who is known as a prolific underground vendor of compromised RDP servers, has begun operating CrySis/Dharma ransomware.

This report covers information provided on May 31, 2017, by a sensitive and reliable source of ours, who has visibility into lamba’s infrastructure and resources.

The source reported the following:
---
Having analyzed some of lamba’s private Jabber chats, which I discovered on one of his servers, I learnt the following:

1) in early Feb. 2017, lamba was communicating with the Jabber ID bars228@exploit. Apparently, lamba had hired actor bars228 to develop a cryptolocker-type ransomware, which he was then dropping to compromised RDP servers he gained access to. The conversation indicates that the quality of the cryptolocker was not good and that bars228 was evading talking to lamba for some time. lamba has also provided the following link: https://decrypter.emsisoft.com/opentoyou, claiming that bars228 can learn from there what errors in code were made, indicating that the ransomware they were discussing was apparently the OpenToYou cryptolocker.

lamba has also stated that despite quality issues, he was quite successful in infecting compromised RDPs with the ransomware, as on average 2-5 victims out of 100 paid the ransom.

The conversation further indicates that bars223 was also involved in carding fraud, using lamba’s RDP servers to buy goods with compromised credit cards. 2) in mid-February 2017, lamba was looking for a keylogging tool to plant on some compromised RDP servers. It looks like that he ended up buying ja_far’s keylogger. Lamba has also used his malware crypting services several times, making payments to the following Bitcoin address: 1DwvnBfgbe1xF3EB1YJJJqbjcJuqR4H1bo.

3) Lamba’s chats indicate that he sold some of his RDP credentials to the owner of the RDP shop rdpterminals. The two communicated via the Jabber IDs seller.rdpwalmart@pandion.im and seller.rdpwalmart@swissjabber.ch.

4) in late March 2017, lamba communicated with his partner socksbox via the Jabber IDs sbsupport@exploit.im and tssupport@exploit.im. The partnership of the two resulted in the following:

- lamba purchased socksbox’s RDP checker, claiming that the one he was using at that moment was of inferior quality. As the chat contained the link to the checker’s binary, I was able to download it [researcher’s comment: the checker may be found attached];

- lamba was using socksbox’s bulletproof hosting services, purchasing at least one VDS - 91.195.103.166 (CJSC Metrostandart, Prague, Czech Republic). The chat also contained a link to the server management panel, which was located at 134.19.181.75 (Global Layer B.V., Alblasserdam, Netherlands).

Lamba was paying socksbox at the following Bitcoin address: 17nqW53Z7tNdNUpxVNd65Kw8SVC9NcDNDr.

Chat logs also reveal that socksbox offered lamba to join his botnet campaign, claiming that he had a licensed Floki bot and a pack of web-injects targeting US victims.

The following are our comments to each of the points outlined by our source:


1. As we reported earlier, since late Feb. 2017 lamba has been operating the CrySis/Dharma ransomware. This new intelligence indicates that this was not the only ransomware campaign the actor was involved in: the chat logs obtained by our source point to OpenToYou cryptolocker, a piece of ransomware which was apparently developed and/or modified by actor bars228 for lamba’s needs.
   
Additional information about the OpenToYou ransomware may be found in the following blog article by PCrisk: https://www.pcrisk.com/removal-guides/10807-opentoyou-ransomware.

2. Ja_far is known to us as a Ukrainian actor engaged in the development of various malware and hacking tools, including ransomware, malware loaders, key logging tools, password stealers, etc. This actor was earlier extensively covered in a series of corresponding reports (see the Similar Reports section).


3. Based on the data provided by our source, we managed to associate lamba with the handle krot1, which he has been using to sell RDP credentials via the RDP shop Rdpterminals. This shop is run by an actor known under the same handle, rdpterminals, and at various times was known to have used the following domain names:

4. Actor socksbox (aka tunastock, vo0doo, vo0doos, vo0doo-1 ) is an actor who is known to be behind a new SOCKS proxy service called tunastock.ru (see Similar Reports section).
